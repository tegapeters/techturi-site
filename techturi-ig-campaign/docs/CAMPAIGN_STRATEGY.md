# Techturi Instagram Launch Campaign

30-day campaign plan for the techturi.org launch. Daily posting, no face required, balanced across launch / learners / clients.

---

## The strategy in one paragraph

You just launched a free learning library AND a paid web studio. Most launch campaigns fail because they shout "we exist!" for a week and then go silent. This one doesn't. The structure: **week 1 announces, week 2 teaches, week 3 shows proof, week 4 pitches.** Every post either educates (builds trust with learners) or demonstrates (builds trust with clients). No selfies, no motivational quotes, no engagement bait — just useful content that happens to end with "techturi.org in bio."

---

## Content pillars (pick each post from one)

| Pillar | What it is | % of posts |
|---|---|---|
| **Roadmaps** | Skill trees, learning paths, "how to break into X" carousels | 35% |
| **Receipts** | Real tools, real dashboards, real screenshots from your work | 25% |
| **Services** | Wix tier explainers, web build showcases, pricing transparency | 20% |
| **Commentary** | Hot takes on tech careers, industry observations (your POV) | 15% |
| **Meta** | Behind-the-scenes of Techturi itself (launch, tech stack, decisions) | 5% |

---

## Content format mix

All posts are designed for static creation (no face, no video recording required):

- **Carousels (10 slides)** — 40% of posts. High engagement, educational depth.
- **Single posts** — 30%. Quotes, hooks, single-stat reveals.
- **Reels (typography animation, screen recording)** — 20%. Built as HTML/CSS animations you screen-record in OBS or with macOS/Windows built-in tools.
- **Stories** — daily, separate from the grid. Polls, quizzes, countdowns, re-shares of main posts.

## Posting rhythm

- **Grid post:** once per day, between 11am and 1pm CT (lunch scroll) OR 7pm–9pm CT (evening scroll). Pick one window and stick with it.
- **Stories:** 2–4 per day. Re-share the day's post to stories immediately after posting. Add polls/questions throughout the day. Countdown stickers for upcoming content.
- **Reels:** the 4 reels above should post in the main feed AND as reels (both checkboxes when uploading).

## Story template plan (daily, takes 5 minutes)

Use these 5 recurring story types throughout the campaign:

1. **"New post is up"** — re-share the day's grid post with a circle/arrow sticker
2. **Poll/Quiz** — one per day, related to the day's topic. E.g. "What's harder to learn: SQL or Python?" Poll sticker.
3. **"Save for later"** — remind people they can save the carousel. Use the countdown sticker.
4. **Behind the scenes** — a screenshot of something you're working on (a dashboard, code, a notebook). No face, just the screen.
5. **Link sticker** — once per day minimum. Send to techturi.org or a specific track page.

## What to do with DMs and comments

- Respond to every DM in the first week, even one-word ones. Instagram's algorithm rewards it.
- If someone asks "how do I start" → send them to `techturi.org/learn`, NOT a generic list in the DM.
- If someone asks about services → "Happy to jump on a 15-min call — techturi.org/book" (don't pitch in DMs).
- Pin 2–3 top comments on each post (yours or kind replies from others). Helps engagement signal.

---

## What to measure

Don't drown in metrics. Track these 5 only, weekly:

1. **Profile visits** (from Instagram insights)
2. **Website clicks** from the bio link
3. **Saves per post** (the best organic reach signal)
4. **New followers**
5. **DMs received** (these are your warmest leads)

Set a goal for month 1: **500 website clicks from Instagram, 10 DM inquiries.** If you hit that, you're on track.

---

## What to skip

- Don't run paid ads until you have 30+ posts and can see what organic content performs. Then boost the winners.
- Don't post motivational quotes unless you're quoting yourself from an actual lesson.
- Don't follow-for-follow or engagement pods. Algorithm sniffs them out.
- Don't chase trending audio unless it genuinely fits the post. Typography Reels don't need trending audio — they need clean sound design.
- Don't post on weekends until you know your audience is active then. Most tech/career accounts are weekday accounts.

---

## The one rule to break if tempted

If a post doesn't teach, show proof, or have a clear CTA → don't post it. "Cute" engagement-bait content dilutes the brand. Every Techturi post should earn its place.
